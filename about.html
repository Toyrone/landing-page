About EpidAlert

(formerly EbolaAlert)

Inspired by Ebola outbreak in West Africa

2014

EpidAlert (formerly EbolaAlert) is a Nigerian not-for-profit applying some of its innovations and lessons from the Ebola response to the wider business of disease prevention, detection and response to foster global health security. EbolaAlert served as a glue in the command and control of Ebola in Nigeria and also ranked high in its unique use of technologies such as social media, telephony, and other digital services.

And the need to
sustain the public health response momentum

Nigeria's Ebola response was a strategic timestamp in the country's Public Health development milestone. Lessons learned must be sustained across all institutions.

EpidAlert's Vision

After more than a decade of effort, the NHAct was approved by the National Assembly in 2013 and signed into law by the president in 2014.It was gazetted in 2016 but has never been funded. The NHAct stipulates that 1% of consolidated federal revenues be set aside to support the BHCPF. 50% of the funds are allocated to the National Health Insurace Scheme (NHIS) 'gateway', 45% through the NAtional Primary Health Care Development Agency (NPHCDA) 'gateway', and 5% are aside to deal with emergencies.
